# Pathment Suggestions

This is our shared working document for Pathment. It is where we keep track of
the suggestions we have talked through together, what we have already put into
the product, and what we are still thinking about. We add to it as we go, and
anyone can leave a comment or a new thought anywhere on it.

It is not a spec and it is not final. It is the living record of the
conversation we are all having about where Pathment goes next.

## What we have covered so far

- **Mentor setup and guidance:** a short first-run onboarding for a new mentor
  (profile, an AI key, a schedule to adopt, and 1:1 availability), plus a mentor
  spec and FAQs for what a mentor actually does and how.
- **The day, the talks, and the schedule:** three talks on three meals (Mindset
  at breakfast, Engineering at lunch, Dean at dinner), morning journaling and
  reading, weekday structure versus the weekend grind plus family time, and
  talks patched onto meals on purpose as habit stacking.
- **Schedules and how work flows:** build, assign, and edit custom schedules,
  fill each slot per mentee with a roadmap or a recurring task, roadmaps as
  templates that auto-assign the work step by step and can be chained, and a
  cleaner one-step way to link a task to a slot.
- **Reports and communication:** AI generated weekly and monthly reports, weekly
  encouraging emails based on the stats (for example the best member of each
  clan), one announcements channel to move off scattered chats like WhatsApp,
  and checklists for specific kinds of work (an article, branding, notes), which
  today lives partly inside task approval criteria.
- **Specialized clans:** clans organized by technology and by level, with
  communities inside a clan.
- **Gamification:** a leaderboard, XP, levels and streaks, gifts, incentives,
  referrals, a path into a talent network, and co-mentees for specific access
  (today partly covered by collaborators and cross-clan access).
- **The human level:** a progress score over a chosen time window, absolute
  versus relative progress so people are measured fairly, daily logs with a
  quick note per item, a personality read from both the mentor and AI, a mentor
  report and feedback, inviting a psychologist or specialist whose notes are
  attributed to them, and sharing the read carefully and only to help.
- **Cross-clan and governance:** clan change requests with a reason chosen from
  a dropdown, cross-clan assignments (including additional responsibility for a
  clan and co-mentee access), and the policies that govern it all.
- **Mentee experience:** a community feed where mentees cheer each other on,
  share wins, ask questions, and post the odd light internal meme, a My Mentees
  view so a mentor sees everyone in one place, and 1:1 availability with a way
  for a mentee to request a time.
- **Growing the next mentors:** a path from mentee to mentor or co-mentor, a
  brief interview that asks how willing someone is to help others, and then
  approval and promotion.
- **Polish and consistency:** a clean, calm editorial style with no soft glows
  or gradients, em-dashes and AI-ish characters removed from the copy, and fixes
  to card padding and to mentee-facing copy that had read like mentor-facing
  copy.

## What we are still thinking about

Open threads. Nothing decided yet, just things on our mind. Add to these or
start a new one.

- Grouping the long mentor sidebar into sections like Core, Growth, and Org, so
  it is not one long list.
- Letting a mentor fill a slot with the same roadmap or recurring task for
  several mentees at once, rather than one at a time.
- Persisting community posts, announcements, and promotions so they survive a
  reload and can drive real notifications and emails.
- Whether a one-click jump to a specific roadmap step should come back for power
  users, or whether stepping forward and back is enough.
