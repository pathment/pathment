# Pathment Suggestions

This is our shared working document for Pathment. It is where we keep track of
the suggestions we have talked through together, what we have already put into
the product, and what we are still thinking about. We add to it as we go, and
anyone can leave a comment or a new thought anywhere on it.

It is not a spec and it is not final. It is the living record of the
conversation we are all having about where Pathment goes next.

## What we have covered so far

### Mentor setup and guidance

A short first-run onboarding for a new mentor, profile, an AI key, a schedule to
adopt, and 1:1 availability, so nobody starts cold. Alongside it, a mentor spec
and FAQs that lay out what a mentor actually does and how.

### The day, the talks, and the schedule

The heart of the method. Three talks ride on three meals: the Mindset talk at
breakfast, the Engineering talk at lunch, and the Dean talk at dinner. Morning
journaling and reading sit at the front of the day. Weekdays carry the
structured day, and the weekend is the long grind plus family time. We patch the
talks onto meals on purpose, this is habit stacking, the meal is the anchor that
makes the talk automatic.

On top of that structure, a mentor can build, assign, and edit custom schedules,
and fill each slot per mentee with a roadmap or a recurring task. Roadmaps are
templates that auto-assign the work step by step, they can be chained, and we
made linking a task to a slot one clean step instead of several.

### Reports and communication

AI generated weekly and monthly reports. Weekly encouraging emails based on the
stats, for example celebrating the best member of each clan. One announcements
channel so we can move off scattered chats like WhatsApp. We also talked about
checklists for specific kinds of work, an article, branding, notes, which today
lives partly inside task approval criteria.

### Specialized clans

Clans organized by technology and by level, with communities inside a clan so
people can find their group.

### Gamification

A leaderboard, XP, levels, and streaks. Gifts, incentives, and referrals. A path
into a talent network. We also discussed co-mentees for specific access, which
today is partly covered by collaborators and cross-clan access.

### The human level

This is what makes Pathment different. A progress score over a chosen time
window. Absolute versus relative progress so people are measured fairly. Daily
logs with a quick note per item. A personality read from both the mentor and AI,
a mentor report and feedback, and the ability to invite a psychologist or
specialist whose notes are attributed to them. The personality read is shared
and controlled carefully, and only ever used to help.

### Cross-clan and governance

Clan change requests with a reason chosen from a dropdown. Cross-clan
assignments, including giving a clan additional responsibility and granting
co-mentee access. And the policies that govern clan changes, cross-clan work,
and promotion.

### Mentee experience

A community feed where mentees cheer each other on, share wins, ask questions,
and post the odd light internal meme, mentees motivating mentees. A My Mentees
view so a mentor can see everyone in one place. And 1:1 availability the mentor
publishes, with a way for a mentee to request a time.

### Growing the next mentors

A path from mentee to mentor or co-mentor, including a brief interview that asks
how willing someone is to help others, and then approval and promotion.

### Polish and consistency

A clean, calm editorial style with no soft glows or gradients. We removed
em-dashes and AI-ish characters from the copy, fixed card padding so text is not
flush to the edges, and fixed mentee-facing copy that had been reading like
mentor-facing copy.

## What we are still thinking about

Open threads. Nothing decided yet, just things on our mind. Add to these or
start a new one.

- Grouping the long mentor sidebar into sections like Core, Growth, and Org, so
  it is not one long list.
- Letting a mentor fill a slot with the same roadmap or recurring task for
  several mentees at once, rather than one at a time.
- Persisting community posts, announcements, and promotions so they survive a
  reload and can drive real notifications and emails.
- Whether a one-click jump to a specific roadmap step should come back for power
  users, or whether stepping forward and back is enough.
